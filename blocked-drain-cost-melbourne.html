Why Blocked Drain Costs Vary So Much in Melbourne

One plumber quotes $150. Another quotes $600. Same suburb, same symptom — blocked drain. The difference isn't always skill or quality; it's usually what's actually happening inside the pipe and what equipment is needed to fix it properly.

A slow shower drain caused by a hair clog near the surface is a completely different job to a main sewer line blocked by tree roots 15 metres down. The tools, time, and expertise required are not the same, and neither is the price.

The key factors that determine what your blocked drain will cost in Melbourne are below.

📍 Depth of the Blockage

A surface blockage in a shower trap is fast to clear. A blockage deep in the main sewer line requires more powerful equipment and more time on-site. Deeper always costs more.

🌳 Tree Root Intrusion

Root-blocked drains in Melbourne's eastern suburbs — Montrose, Croydon, Ferntree Gully — almost always need hydro jetting, not just snaking. Roots grow back if you just punch through them. Proper clearing costs more but lasts much longer.

⚙️ Equipment Required

An electric eel is cheaper to operate than a 5000 PSI hydro jet. But the eel only punches a hole; the jet scours the pipe clean. The right tool depends on the blockage — and a plumber who only brings one tool isn't doing you a favour.

🔍 CCTV Inspection

A camera inspection adds to the upfront cost but saves money long-term. It shows you exactly what caused the blockage and whether the pipe is structurally damaged — information you need before spending on repeated clears.

🕐 Time of Day

Business hours are always the most cost-effective. Evening, weekend, and public holiday call-outs carry a surcharge. If it's safe to wait until morning, it will cost less.

🚪 Site Access

If your inspection opening is easy to reach, the job is straightforward. If a plumber has to locate a buried pit, lift pavers, or work in a confined space, that takes longer and costs more.

Hydro Jetting vs Electric Eel — Which One Do You Actually Need?

This is the most common question about drain pricing in Melbourne, and it matters because the two methods are priced differently.

Electric Eel (Drain Snake)

A rotating cable that cuts or pushes through a blockage. It's faster to set up and lower cost. For a simple hair or grease clog in a single fixture — shower, basin, kitchen sink — it does the job. But it only opens a path through the blockage, it doesn't clean the pipe walls. For tree roots, it's a temporary fix at best.

High-Pressure Hydro Jet

A 5000 PSI water jet that scours the full internal diameter of the pipe clean. It removes root mass, grease build-up, scale, and debris from the pipe walls — not just the centre. The results last significantly longer. For main sewer lines, recurring blockages, or any job with root intrusion, hydro jetting is the correct tool. A plumber who only offers snaking on a root-blocked sewer line is not fixing your problem.

In Melbourne's eastern suburbs — Montrose, Mooroolbark, Ringwood, Ferntree Gully — the majority of main sewer blockages involve tree roots. Hydro jetting is the standard method for these jobs, not an optional upgrade.

When Do You Need a CCTV Drain Inspection?

A CCTV drain camera inspection lets your plumber see inside the pipe before and after clearing. It identifies the exact cause of the blockage and shows whether the pipe itself is structurally sound. It's not always necessary — but it's essential in these situations:

  • Recurring blockages — if you've had the same drain cleared more than once in 12 months, the camera shows you why it keeps happening.
  • Pre-purchase property inspection — a CCTV drain inspection before buying reveals cracked, collapsed, or root-invaded pipes that could cost thousands to repair.
  • After a major main-line blockage — confirms the pipe is intact and uncovers any structural damage caused by the blockage.
  • Gurgling or sewage smell — signs of a deeper issue that a surface clear won't fix.
  • Insurance claims — insurers accept CCTV footage as evidence of pipe condition or cause of damage.

At Water Serpent, every main sewer line job ends with a camera run. You see the footage. You know the pipe is clear, or you know exactly what needs to be done next — before you spend another dollar.

How to Avoid Getting Overcharged for a Blocked Drain

The Melbourne plumbing market has some operators who take advantage of urgent situations. Here's how to protect yourself:

✅ Get a Fixed Quote First

Any reputable plumber will give you a written fixed price or clear estimate before touching your pipes. "We'll see how we go" is not a quote — it's an open invoice.

✅ Ask About Call-Out Fees

Some plumbers advertise a low service price but add a separate call-out fee on arrival. Ask upfront. Water Serpent charges no call-out fee during business hours.

✅ Ask What's Included

Does the price include hydro jetting, or just an electric eel? Is CCTV extra? What happens if it doesn't clear first attempt? Know what you're paying for before they start.

✅ Don't Rush Into Excavation

If a plumber recommends excavation after a single blockage, ask to see the CCTV footage first. Pipe relining is often far less expensive and less invasive than digging. Get a second opinion if in doubt.

✅ Check They're Licensed

Any plumber working in Victoria must hold a current BPC registration. Ask for it. Unlicensed plumbing work is illegal and won't be covered by your home insurance.

✅ Avoid Harsh Chemical Drain Cleaners

Caustic drain cleaners damage pipe joints, are dangerous to handle, and don't fix root or structural blockages. They also make the plumber's job harder — which adds to your bill.

Ongoing Maintenance — The Cost-Effective Alternative

For properties with recurring drain issues — homes with heavy tree canopy, restaurants, strata buildings, and rental properties — a scheduled maintenance plan is significantly cheaper than repeated emergency call-outs.

A regular hydro jet service keeps drains clear year-round, extends pipe life, and means you never face an after-hours emergency bill. Water Serpent offers maintenance plans for residential, property management, and commercial clients across Melbourne's eastern suburbs — quarterly, bi-monthly, or monthly depending on your property's needs.

Blocked Drain Cost FAQs — Melbourne 2026

How much does it cost to unblock a drain in Melbourne in 2026?

Most residential blocked drains in Melbourne cost between $250 and $450 for a standard clear. Jobs requiring hydro jetting, tree root cutting, or CCTV inspection sit higher. The best protection is a plumber who gives you a fixed written quote before any work begins — which is exactly what Water Serpent does.

Why is my blocked drain quote higher than I expected?

The main cost drivers are the depth of the blockage in the main sewer line, tree root intrusion requiring hydro jetting instead of snaking, after-hours or weekend timing, and access difficulty to your inspection opening. A simple shower drain is always cheaper than a blocked main sewer line.

Is hydro jetting worth the extra cost?

Yes — especially for recurring blockages or tree root problems. A hydro jet scours the full pipe wall clean, while an electric eel only punches a hole through the blockage. Hydro jetting lasts significantly longer between clears and is the industry standard for main sewer lines in Melbourne's eastern suburbs.

Do I need a CCTV drain inspection?

You need one when drains block repeatedly, when you're buying a property, or after a major main-line blockage. The camera shows exactly why it's blocking — roots, cracks, collapsed pipe — so you fix the cause, not just the symptom. Water Serpent runs the camera on every main sewer job at no extra charge.

Are there call-out fees on top of the job price?

Water Serpent Plumbing charges no call-out fee during business hours. After-hours emergency response carries a surcharge, which we tell you upfront before attending. Always ask any plumber about call-out fees before they arrive.

What is pipe relining and when is it needed?

Pipe relining is a no-dig method of repairing cracked, root-damaged, or collapsed pipes from the inside. It's needed when a CCTV inspection reveals structural damage that clearing alone won't fix. It carries a 50-year warranty and avoids costly excavation of your yard or driveway.

Will my insurance cover a blocked drain?

Standard home insurance usually doesn't cover gradual blockages, but may cover damage caused by sewage overflow — flooring, walls, carpets. We provide CCTV reports and written quotes that insurers accept as evidence of pipe condition and cause of damage.
