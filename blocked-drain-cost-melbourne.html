Why Blocked Drain Costs Vary So Much in Melbourne

One plumber quotes $150. Another quotes $600. Same suburb, same symptom — blocked drain. The difference isn’t always skill or quality; it’s usually what’s actually happening inside the pipe and what equipment is needed to fix it properly.

A slow shower drain caused by a hair clog near the surface is a completely different job to a main sewer line blocked by tree roots 15 metres down. The tools, time and expertise required are not the same, and neither is the price.

📍 Depth of the Blockage

A surface blockage in a shower trap is fast to clear. A blockage deep in the main sewer line needs more powerful equipment and more time on site. Deeper always costs more.

🌳 Tree Root Intrusion

Root-blocked drains in Melbourne’s eastern suburbs — Montrose, Croydon, Ferntree Gully — almost always need hydro jetting, not just snaking. Roots grow back if you just punch through them.

⚙️ Equipment Required

An electric eel is cheaper to run than a 5000 PSI jetter, but it only punches a hole; the jet scours the pipe clean. The right tool depends on the blockage — a plumber who only brings one tool isn’t doing you a favour.

🔍 CCTV Inspection

A camera inspection shows exactly what caused the blockage and whether the pipe is structurally damaged — information you need before spending on repeated clears.

🕐 Time of Day

Business hours are always the most cost-effective. Evening, weekend and public holiday callouts carry a surcharge — we tell you upfront, before attending, not on the invoice.

🚪 Site Access

An easy-to-reach inspection opening keeps the job simple. Locating a buried pit, lifting pavers or working in a confined space takes longer and costs more — pipe locating finds it fast.

Hydro Jetting vs Electric Eel — Which One Do You Need?

This is the most common question about drain pricing in Melbourne, and it matters because the two methods are priced differently.

🐍 Electric Eel (Drain Snake)

A rotating cable that cuts or pushes through a blockage. Faster to set up and lower cost. For a simple hair or grease clog in a single fixture — shower, basin, kitchen sink — it does the job. But it only opens a path through the blockage; it doesn’t clean the pipe walls. For tree roots, it’s a temporary fix at best.

💦 High-Pressure Hydro Jet

A 5000 PSI water jet that scours the full internal diameter of the pipe clean. It removes root mass, grease, scale and debris from the pipe walls — not just the centre. The results last significantly longer. For main sewer lines, recurring blockages or any job with root intrusion, hydro jetting is the correct tool. A plumber who only offers snaking on a root-blocked sewer line is not fixing your problem.

In Melbourne’s eastern suburbs — Montrose, Mooroolbark, Ringwood, Ferntree Gully — the majority of main sewer blockages involve tree roots. Hydro jetting is the standard method for these jobs, not an optional upgrade.

When Do You Need a CCTV Drain Inspection?

A CCTV drain camera inspection lets your plumber see inside the pipe before and after clearing. It’s not always necessary — but it’s essential in these situations:

  • Recurring blockages — cleared more than once in 12 months? The camera shows you why it keeps happening.
  • Pre-purchase inspection — reveals cracked, collapsed or root-invaded pipes before you buy. See our pre-purchase guide.
  • After a major main-line blockage — confirms the pipe is intact.
  • Gurgling or sewer smells — signs of a deeper issue a surface clear won’t fix. Smoke testing finds odour escape points cameras can’t see.
  • Insurance claims — insurers accept CCTV footage as evidence of pipe condition and cause of damage.

What it costs with us: every main sewer job ends with a camera run at no extra charge. You see the footage, you know the pipe is clear — or you know exactly what needs fixing before you spend another dollar. Standalone inspections are quoted upfront based on line length and access.

After-Hours & Emergency Costs — The Honest Version

Evening, weekend and public holiday callouts cost more everywhere — the question is whether you find out before or after the work. We tell you the full price on the phone, before attending. A few honest tips:

  • If it can safely wait until morning, it will cost less. One blocked fixture that isn’t overflowing can usually wait; sewage coming up through floor wastes cannot.
  • Stop using water immediately — it keeps a bad situation from becoming a flooded-floor situation (and a bigger bill).
  • Don’t pour chemicals in while you wait. They rarely work, and a drain full of caustic solution is slower and more hazardous to clear — which costs you more.

For genuine emergencies — sewage overflow, every fixture backed up — our emergency blocked drain service runs 24/7 with same-day response across Melbourne’s east.

How to Avoid Getting Overcharged

✅ Get a Fixed Quote First

Any reputable plumber will give you a written fixed price before touching your pipes. “We’ll see how we go” is not a quote — it’s an open invoice.

✅ Ask About Call-Out Fees

Some plumbers advertise a low service price then add a call-out fee on arrival. Ask upfront. Water Serpent charges no call-out fee during business hours.

✅ Ask What’s Included

Does the price include jetting, or just an eel? Is CCTV extra? What happens if it doesn’t clear first attempt? Know before they start.

✅ Don’t Rush Into Excavation

If a plumber recommends digging after a single blockage, ask to see the CCTV footage first. Pipe relining is often far less expensive and less invasive.

✅ Check They’re Licensed

Any plumber working in Victoria must hold current registration. Ask for it. Unlicensed work is illegal and voids your home insurance.

✅ Skip Chemical Cleaners

Caustic cleaners damage pipe joints, are dangerous to handle, and don’t fix root or structural blockages. They also make the plumber’s job harder — which adds to your bill.

7 Ways to Keep Drain Costs Down

  1. Act at the gurgle stage. A partial blockage is a smaller job than a full overflow — every time.
  2. Book business hours when it’s safe to wait.
  3. Know where your inspection opening is — easy access saves time on site.
  4. Skip the supermarket chemicals — they add hazard, not value.
  5. Bundle small jobs — a dripping tap fixed during a drain visit costs less than a separate callout. See general plumbing.
  6. Get the camera proof — footage means you never pay twice for the same misdiagnosed problem.
  7. Consider maintenance if you’ve had two or more blockages in a year — a scheduled jetting service beats emergency pricing every time.

Blocked Drain Cost FAQs — Melbourne 2026

How much does it cost to unblock a drain in Melbourne in 2026?

Most residential blocked drains in Melbourne cost between $300 and $800, depending on the issue. Simple single-fixture clears sit at the lower end; hydro jetting, tree root cutting and deep main-line work sit toward the top. The best protection is a plumber who gives you a fixed written quote before any work begins — which is exactly what Water Serpent does.

Why is my blocked drain quote higher than I expected?

The main cost drivers are the depth of the blockage in the main sewer line, tree root intrusion requiring hydro jetting instead of snaking, after-hours or weekend timing, and access difficulty to your inspection opening. A simple shower drain is always cheaper than a blocked main sewer line.

Is hydro jetting worth the extra cost?

Yes — especially for recurring blockages or tree root problems. A hydro jet scours the full pipe wall clean, while an electric eel only punches a hole through the blockage. Hydro jetting lasts significantly longer between clears and is the industry standard for main sewer lines in Melbourne’s eastern suburbs.

How much does a CCTV drain inspection cost in Melbourne?

With Water Serpent, the camera run is included at no extra charge on every main sewer clearing job. Standalone CCTV inspections — pre-purchase checks, recurring blockage diagnosis — are quoted upfront based on the length of the line and access, before any work starts.

How much does hydro jetting cost in Melbourne?

More than a basic eel clear, less than paying to clear the same drain three times a year. Most jetting jobs on residential lines still land within the standard $300–$800 range depending on roots and line length — always as a fixed upfront quote, never an hourly open tab.

Do plumbers charge for quotes in Melbourne?

Some do. We don’t — describe the symptoms over the phone and Jack will diagnose the likely cause and give you a fixed price for free, usually in about two minutes on 0425 226 636.

Do I need a CCTV drain inspection?

You need one when drains block repeatedly, when you’re buying a property, or after a major main-line blockage. The camera shows exactly why it’s blocking — roots, cracks, collapsed pipe — so you fix the cause, not just the symptom. Water Serpent runs the camera on every main sewer job at no extra charge.

Are there call-out fees on top of the job price?

Water Serpent Plumbing charges no call-out fee during business hours. After-hours emergency response carries a surcharge, which we tell you upfront before attending. Always ask any plumber about call-out fees before they arrive.

Are blocked drains more expensive to clear in winter?

The job price doesn’t change with the season, but winter exposes problems: partially blocked stormwater and sewer lines that coped in dry weather overflow in the first heavy rain, and demand for emergency slots peaks. Clearing a slow drain in autumn is the cheapest version of that job you’ll ever get.

What is pipe relining and when is it needed?

Pipe relining is a no-dig method of repairing cracked, root-damaged or collapsed pipes from the inside. It’s needed when a CCTV inspection reveals structural damage that clearing alone won’t fix. It carries a 50-year design life and avoids costly excavation of your yard or driveway.

Will my insurance cover a blocked drain?

Standard home insurance usually doesn’t cover gradual blockages, but may cover damage caused by sewage overflow — flooring, walls, carpets. We provide CCTV reports and written quotes that insurers accept as evidence of pipe condition and cause of damage.
