Is It a Drain Emergency? These Are the Signs

  • Sewage backing up inside: waste rising in the shower, floor waste or lowest drain when you flush — stop using water immediately and call.
  • Overflowing gully trap: waste water pooling around the outside grate near the house means the main line is fully blocked.
  • Toilet overflowing when flushed: one flush from flooding the bathroom — this is a blocked toilet emergency, and often a sign of a blocked sewer.
  • Every drain slow or blocked at once: sinks, shower and toilet all struggling together points to the main sewer line, not one pipe.
  • Stormwater flooding in rain: water sheeting towards the house or garage because a stormwater drain can't cope — urgent in a downpour, especially in the Ranges.

What To Do Right Now (Before the Plumber Arrives)

  1. Stop the water: no flushing, no taps, pause the washing machine and dishwasher — every litre has nowhere to go but back up.
  2. Keep clear of the overflow: raw sewage is a health hazard; keep kids and pets away and don't try to mop it into other drains.
  3. Skip the chemical drain cleaner: it almost never clears a real blockage, and it turns the pipe into a hazard for whoever works on it.
  4. Call 0425 226 636: Jack will tell you over the phone — free — what to do for your specific situation and give you an honest arrival window.

What counts as a blocked drain emergency?

Sewage backing up into the house, waste overflowing at the gully trap, every drain blocked at once, a toilet that overflows when flushed, or stormwater flooding towards the house during rain. If waste or water is coming back the wrong way, treat it as an emergency and stop using water immediately.
