Why Drains Block So Often in Upwey

Upwey's terrain works against its drainage. Steep blocks mean stormwater moves fast and carries leaf litter from the native canopy straight into pits and downpipes, while the tall eucalypts that make the suburb beautiful push roots toward any moisture in the ground. Underneath, reactive clay expands and contracts through the wet Ranges winters and dry summers, cracking rigid pipe and opening joints. The most common blocked drain causes we see in Upwey are:

  • Root intrusion from mountain ash, messmate and manna gum into earthenware sewer joints
  • Clay soil movement on sloping blocks cracking old rigid pipe and opening joints
  • Leaf litter and silt overwhelming stormwater pits after heavy Ranges downpours
  • Collapsed or bellied pipe sections that have shifted on Upwey's sloping ground
  • Grease and food waste building up in kitchen drain lines of older homes

Pipe Relining: The Permanent Fix for Root-Prone Upwey Lines

Because root intrusion is so common in Upwey, clearing alone often buys only a few months before the roots return through the same joint. Pipe relining solves it for good: we insert an epoxy liner inside the existing pipe, sealing the joints and creating a smooth, root-resistant surface — all without excavating your garden or driveway. On a sloping Upwey block it avoids the cost and disruption of digging a trench down the hill, and it typically carries a long-life structural warranty.
