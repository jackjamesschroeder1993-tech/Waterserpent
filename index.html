Why Hydro Jetting Beats a Drain Snake

Fast Blocked Basin Clearing

A blocked bathroom basin is one of those problems that starts as an inconvenience and quickly becomes a daily frustration. Standing water in the basin, slow drainage, and bad smells are all signs the drain needs professional attention.

What Blocks a Bathroom Basin?

  • Hair — the most common cause. Hair binds with soap residue to form a dense mat in the p-trap or drain pipe.
  • Soap scum — builds up progressively on pipe walls, narrowing the drain over time.
  • Toothpaste and product residue — accumulates and combines with other debris.

Why Not Just Use Chemical Drain Cleaner?

Chemical drain cleaners are harsh on pipes — particularly older PVC and chrome p-traps — and rarely remove the full blockage. They may temporarily improve flow while leaving significant residue that causes rapid re-blockage. Professional clearing removes the blockage completely.

Blocked Shower Drain Specialists

A blocked shower is more than annoying — water pooling in the shower base can lead to water damage around tiles, grout deterioration, and mould growth if left unaddressed. We clear shower drain blockages properly so the problem doesn't keep coming back.

Why Shower Drains Block

  • Hair accumulation — the number one cause. Long hair in particular forms a dense plug in the drain trap or pipe that water cannot pass through.
  • Soap and shampoo residue — combines with hair to form a solid, sticky blockage.
  • Hard water mineral deposits — over time, calcium and mineral build-up narrows the drain in hard water areas.
  • Body wash and conditioner — thicker products contribute to build-up on drain walls.

When a Shower Blockage Is More Serious

If multiple shower and bath drains in the home are slow simultaneously, or if your shower drain backs up at the same time as other fixtures, this indicates a deeper blockage in the main drain line — not just the individual shower drain. This requires professional diagnosis with a CCTV camera.

Preventing Shower Drain Blockages

A quality hair catcher over the shower drain is the single most effective preventative measure. Clearing it after every shower takes seconds and significantly reduces blockage frequency. We can advise on suitable drain covers during our visit.

Our Shower Drain Service

What Is Drain Smoke Testing?

A drain smoke test identifies leaks, cracks, unsealed joints and illegal connections in a drainage system. Non-toxic smoke is pumped into the drain line under gentle pressure — anywhere it escapes is where the problem is. It is the fastest and most reliable method for tracking down the source of sewage smells that CCTV inspection alone cannot always pinpoint.

When You Need a Drain Smoke Test

  • Sewage or rotten egg smell inside the home with no obvious cause
  • Intermittent drain odours in bathrooms, kitchens or laundries
  • Smell only appears after rain or when drains are used
  • Pre-purchase inspection — checking for illegal stormwater-to-sewer connections
  • After renovations where drain work was carried out
  • Commercial premises with unexplained drain smells

Common Causes Found by Smoke Testing

  • Dry or missing P-traps allowing sewer gas into the home
  • Cracked or broken pipe sections leaking gas
  • Unsealed pipe penetrations through walls or floors
  • Disconnected drain joints
  • Illegal stormwater connections to the sewer
  • Faulty or missing vent pipes

The Smoke Test Process

All drain openings are temporarily sealed, non-toxic smoke is pumped into the drain system, we walk the property observing where smoke appears, and every leak point is documented and quoted for repair. The smoke is completely safe — non-toxic and non-staining.

How to Unblock a Drain: What Works and What Doesn't

May 2025 · 6 min read · By Jack, Water Serpent Plumbing

Home›Blog›How to Unblock a Drain

The Honest Guide to Unblocking a Drain

Searching "how to unblock a drain" is usually the first thing people do when water starts pooling in the sink or shower. As a plumber with 20+ years of experience across Melbourne's South East, I want to give you an honest picture of what actually works — and when you need to call a professional.

Step 1 — Identify What Type of Blockage You Have

Before doing anything, understand what you're dealing with. Is it a single fixture — just the kitchen sink, or just the shower? Or are multiple drains slow at the same time? If multiple drains are affected simultaneously, you have a blockage in the main drain line, not an individual fixture. Stop reading DIY tips and call a plumber — that's a different problem entirely.

For a Single Fixture — What You Can Try

Boiling water — for kitchen sink grease blockages, carefully pour boiling water directly down the drain. This can dissolve fresh grease that hasn't yet hardened. Won't work on older blockages or anything other than grease.

Baking soda and vinegar — the classic combination creates a fizzing reaction that can shift soft blockages. Pour half a cup of baking soda followed by half a cup of white vinegar, wait 20 minutes, then flush with hot water. Works on mild blockages only.

A plunger — the most effective DIY tool for toilet and sink blockages. Use a cup plunger for sinks and a flange plunger for toilets. Create a good seal and use firm, rhythmic pressure. This works well for soft, fresh blockages close to the drain opening.

Drain snake from hardware store — a hand-cranked drain snake can reach further than a plunger and can physically dislodge a blockage. Available from Bunnings for around $20-40. Has limits though — cheap snakes typically only reach 3-5 metres and can't cut through tree roots.

Chemical drain cleaners — avoid them. They're corrosive, they damage older pipes (especially PVC and earthenware), they're environmentally harmful, and they rarely fully clear a blockage. They may improve flow temporarily while leaving significant residue that causes rapid re-blockage.

When DIY Doesn't Work — Call a Plumber

Call a professional when: the blockage keeps coming back in the same drain, multiple fixtures are slow or blocked, there's a sewage smell, you've tried the above and nothing worked, or the blocked drain is in the main sewer line. These situations require professional equipment — a 5000 PSI hydro jetter and CCTV camera — to find the real cause and fix it properly.

Why Does My Drain Smell? Melbourne Plumber Explains

April 2025 · 5 min read · By Jack, Water Serpent Plumbing

Home›Blog›Why Does My Drain Smell

Drain Smells in Melbourne Homes — The Real Causes

A bad smell coming from a drain is one of the most common complaints we get from homeowners across Melbourne's South East. It's unpleasant, it can be a health concern, and it can be surprisingly hard to find the source. Here are the most common causes and what to do about each one.

1. Dry or Empty P-Trap

The P-trap is the curved section of pipe under sinks, basins and floor drains. It holds a small amount of water that creates a seal against sewer gases. If a drain isn't used regularly — a guest bathroom, a floor drain in a laundry — the water in the trap evaporates and sewer gas comes straight up through the drain. Fix: pour water down the drain to refill the trap. Add a small amount of cooking oil to slow future evaporation.

2. Blocked or Partially Blocked Drain

A build-up of hair, soap, grease or food debris in a drain creates a breeding ground for bacteria that produces that distinctive drain smell. If the smell is accompanied by slow drainage, you likely have a partial blockage. Professional clearing with a hydro jetter removes the organic material completely.

3. Cracked or Broken Pipe Leaking Sewer Gas

A cracked pipe in the wall, floor or underground doesn't just leak water — it leaks sewer gas. This is a more serious problem and requires a CCTV inspection or drain smoke test to locate. If you have a smell that appears and disappears, comes from multiple locations, or seems to come from inside a wall, this may be the cause.

Sewer gas contains hydrogen sulphide which has a rotten egg smell. In high concentrations it's a health hazard. If you have a persistent strong sewage smell inside your home, don't ignore it — have it professionally investigated.

4. Tree Root Intrusion

Tree roots in drain pipes allow sewer gas to escape through pipe joint gaps. The smell is typically outside or under the house, and is often accompanied by slow drainage. CCTV inspection will confirm root intrusion.

5. Blocked Vent Pipe

Plumbing vent pipes run through the roof to release sewer gases outside. If these become blocked — by debris, birds nests or leaves — gases are forced down through drain fixtures instead. A blocked vent is often identified by gurgling sounds from toilets and drains alongside the smell.

When to Call a Drain Smoke Test

If you've ruled out the simple causes above and the smell persists, a drain smoke test is the most accurate way to find the source. We pump non-toxic smoke into the drain system and follow it to exactly where it escapes. Call Jack on 0425 226 636.

Hydro Jet vs Drain Snake: Which Actually Works?

The Honest Difference Between Hydro Jetting and Drain Snaking

When you call a plumber about a blocked drain, the method they use matters enormously. Two plumbers attending the same blocked drain may use very different approaches — and the result can vary from a temporary fix that lasts six months to a proper clear that lasts years.

What Is a Drain Snake?

A drain snake (also called an electric eel or auger) is a long, flexible cable that is fed into the drain pipe and rotated to mechanically break through the blockage. It physically punches a hole through the obstruction — but it does not clean the pipe walls or remove all the material causing the blockage.

What Is Hydro Jetting?

Hydro jetting uses water under extremely high pressure — in our case, 5000 PSI — to flush through the drain pipe. The force is strong enough to cut through tree roots, blast away grease, and scour the pipe walls completely clean. It doesn't just punch through the blockage — it removes it entirely.

"A drain snake is like poking a hole in a clogged bathtub. Hydro jetting is like power washing the inside of the pipe from one end to the other."

Why Hydro Jetting Produces Better Long-Term Results

  • Removes all blockage material rather than just displacing it
  • Cleans pipe walls, eliminating the residue that causes re-blockage
  • Cuts through tree roots rather than just bending around them
  • Works on grease and fat build-up that snakes cannot remove
  • The result can be verified with CCTV camera after jetting

When Might a Drain Snake Be Used?

Drain snakes are appropriate for simple, fresh blockages — a foreign object recently dropped into a drain, or a very superficial soft clog close to the access point. For anything recurring, involving tree roots, grease build-up or whole-house drainage issues, hydro jetting is the correct tool.

Kitchen Grease: The Slow Killer of Drain Pipes

Kitchen sink blockages are almost entirely preventable — but they remain one of the most common plumbing callouts across Melbourne. The culprit, in most cases, is fat, oil and grease poured down the sink or washed off dishes.

What Happens to Grease in Your Pipes

Hot grease or oil flows easily when warm — but as it travels through the pipe and cools, it solidifies and sticks to pipe walls. Over weeks and months, this build-up narrows the pipe progressively until water can no longer pass. At that point, no amount of running hot water will shift it — it requires professional clearing.

Never pour cooking oil, fat or grease down the sink. Let it cool, pour it into a container, and put it in the bin. This one habit prevents the majority of kitchen drain blockages.

What to Do About Kitchen Grease Build-Up

If your kitchen sink is draining slowly, don't reach for chemical drain cleaners — they rarely fully remove grease and can damage pipes. The right solution is professional hydro jet cleaning, which scours the pipe walls clean. Call Jack on 0425 226 636.

What Is Pipe Relining?

Pipe relining is a modern, no-dig method of permanently repairing damaged, cracked or root-infiltrated drain pipes from the inside — without excavating your garden, driveway or flooring. A flexible resin liner is inserted into the damaged pipe, inflated to fit perfectly against the pipe walls, and cured to form a brand new pipe within the old one.

The result is a seamless, jointless pipe that is actually stronger than the original — and comes with a long-term product warranty. It is the permanent solution to recurring drain problems caused by ageing or damaged pipes.
