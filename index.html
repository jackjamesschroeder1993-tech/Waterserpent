Common Causes of Blocked Drains in Melbourne

5000 PSI Hydro Jet Drain Cleaning

High-pressure water jetting — or hydro jetting — is the most effective method for clearing stubborn drain blockages. Using water at 5000 PSI, our industrial jetter cuts through tree roots, breaks apart grease build-up, and scours pipe walls clean in a way that drain snakes simply cannot.

Why Hydro Jetting Beats a Drain Snake

A drain snake (or electric eel) physically punches through a blockage — but it leaves residue on pipe walls and can push debris further down. Hydro jetting flushes the entire pipe section, removing all build-up and significantly reducing re-blockage rates.

What Hydro Jetting Clears

  • Tree root intrusion in drain lines
  • Years of accumulated grease and fat
  • Mineral scale and calcium deposits
  • Sludge and bio-film in sewer pipes
  • Stormwater debris and sediment

Fast Blocked Basin Clearing

A blocked bathroom basin is one of those problems that starts as an inconvenience and quickly becomes a daily frustration. Standing water in the basin, slow drainage, and bad smells are all signs the drain needs professional attention.

What Blocks a Bathroom Basin?

  • Hair — the most common cause. Hair binds with soap residue to form a dense mat in the p-trap or drain pipe.
  • Soap scum — builds up progressively on pipe walls, narrowing the drain over time.
  • Toothpaste and product residue — accumulates and combines with other debris.
  • Foreign objects — jewellery, bottle caps and small items accidentally dropped in.

Why Not Just Use Chemical Drain Cleaner?

Chemical drain cleaners are harsh on pipes — particularly older PVC and chrome p-traps — and rarely remove the full blockage. They may temporarily improve flow while leaving significant residue that causes rapid re-blockage. Professional clearing removes the blockage completely.

Our Basin Clearing Process

We access the drain, clear the blockage completely, check the p-trap and associated drain line, and confirm full flow before leaving. Most basin blockages are cleared in under 30 minutes. We leave the area clean and tidy.

Blocked Shower Drain Specialists

A blocked shower is more than annoying — water pooling in the shower base can lead to water damage around tiles, grout deterioration, and mould growth if left unaddressed. We clear shower drain blockages properly so the problem doesn't keep coming back.

Why Shower Drains Block

  • Hair accumulation — the number one cause. Long hair in particular forms a dense plug in the drain trap or pipe that water cannot pass through.
  • Soap and shampoo residue — combines with hair to form a solid, sticky blockage.
  • Hard water mineral deposits — over time, calcium and mineral build-up narrows the drain in hard water areas.
  • Body wash and conditioner — thicker products contribute to build-up on drain walls.

When a Shower Blockage Is More Serious

If multiple shower and bath drains in the home are slow simultaneously, or if your shower drain backs up at the same time as other fixtures, this indicates a deeper blockage in the main drain line — not just the individual shower drain. This requires professional diagnosis with a CCTV camera.

Preventing Shower Drain Blockages

A quality hair catcher over the shower drain is the single most effective preventative measure. Clearing it after every shower takes seconds and significantly reduces blockage frequency. We can advise on suitable drain covers during our visit.

Our Shower Drain Service

We clear the blockage completely — not just partially — and check the associated drain line for any deeper build-up. Same-day service available across Ringwood, Bayswater, Ferntree Gully, Glen Waverley and all South East Melbourne suburbs.

Why the $99 Blocked Drain Plumber Could Cost You Thousands

May 2025 · 6 min read · By Jack, Water Serpent Plumbing

Home›Blog›Why the $99 Plumber Costs You More

The $99 Blocked Drain Special: What They're Not Telling You

You've seen the ads. "$99 Blocked Drain Cleared!" It sounds like a bargain — especially when you've got an overflowing drain and you need help fast. But as a plumber with over 20 years of experience, I've cleaned up after these operators more times than I can count. Here's the honest truth about what's really going on.

"No qualified, properly equipped plumber can attend your home, assess the problem, use professional equipment, and fix it properly for $99. The maths simply doesn't work — and someone always pays the difference. Usually you."

The Reality Behind the $99 Price

Let's be straight about what $99 covers in the real world. A licensed plumber's hourly rate in Melbourne sits well above that figure before you factor in running a vehicle, fuel, tools, insurance, licensing and compliance costs. The moment a qualified tradesperson walks out the door to attend a job, the cost is already more than $99.

So how do these companies advertise it? Simple: the $99 is a call-out and assessment fee designed to get someone in your door. Once they're there, the upselling begins.

The Bait-and-Switch Playbook

Here's what typically happens when a "$99 drain" operator arrives at your home:

  • Step 1 — The assessment: They have a look and immediately identify "additional complications" that push the price well beyond $99. Suddenly it's $350, $500 or more.
  • Step 2 — The urgency pressure: You're told the problem is worse than it looks and needs fixing right now. You're already stressed about the blockage, it's hard to say no.
  • Step 3 — The minimal fix: Even if you pay the inflated price, the work done is often the bare minimum. A quick snake through the drain to temporarily clear the blockage — without addressing the underlying cause.
  • Step 4 — The return visit: Because the root cause wasn't fixed, the blockage returns within weeks or months. You call again. The cycle repeats.

Why Cheap Operators Can't Fix the Real Problem

Properly diagnosing and clearing a blocked drain requires equipment — and quality equipment costs money. A CCTV drain camera to inspect the pipe and identify the actual cause of the blockage. A high-pressure hydro jetter to fully clear root intrusion, grease build-up and debris rather than just punching a hole through it. Pipe locating equipment for accurate diagnosis.

Budget operators often carry none of this. They have a basic drain snake — a tool that physically pushes through a blockage without clearing the pipe walls, without addressing root systems, and without any way to confirm the cause or the quality of the result.

The drain appears to flow after they leave. But the root system is still there. The grease build-up is still coating the pipe walls. The partial collapse causing recurring blockages hasn't been identified. It's a temporary clearance, not a fix.

The Hidden Cost of Cheap Plumbing

Consider the full picture. You pay $99 as a call-out. Then another $400 because of "complications." The drain blocks again six weeks later — another $200 to a different operator. Three months later it's completely blocked again, and this time a camera inspection reveals a tree root intrusion and cracked pipe section that should have been identified in the first visit. Now you're looking at pipe relining or excavation — a job that might cost $3,000–$8,000.

Compare that to a properly equipped plumber who charges a fair, transparent price from the start — uses a CCTV camera to correctly diagnose the issue, clears it properly with a hydro jetter, and tells you honestly what the pipe condition is and what to watch for. The upfront cost is higher. The long-term cost is significantly lower.

What to Look for in a Drain Plumber

  • BPC Registration — check the Plumbing Permit Authority Victoria (PPAV) register. Every plumber working in Victoria must be BPC registered. Ask for their registration number.
  • Proper equipment — do they carry a CCTV drain camera and hydro jetter? If not, they can't properly diagnose or fully clear most blockages.
  • Upfront, transparent pricing — a reputable plumber tells you the full cost before starting work. Not a "$99 special" that doubles on arrival.
  • Owner-operated or identifiable tradesperson — who actually turns up matters. An experienced plumber, not a subcontractor or an apprentice.
  • No pressure tactics — a good plumber assesses the situation and gives you honest options. They don't create urgency or pressure you into decisions on the spot.

Tree Roots in Drains: Melbourne's #1 Cause of Blocked Pipes

May 2025 · 5 min read · By Jack, Water Serpent Plumbing

Home›Blog›Tree Roots in Drains

Why Tree Roots Are Melbourne's Biggest Drain Problem

If you live in Melbourne's South East — suburbs like Ringwood, Bayswater, Ferntree Gully, Lilydale or Croydon — and you've had a blocked drain, there's a very good chance tree roots were involved.

These older, established suburbs have two things in common: mature native trees (gum trees, wattles, liquid ambers) with aggressive root systems, and ageing underground pipe systems — mostly earthenware or early PVC — that were installed decades ago when root intrusion wasn't well understood.

"In Melbourne's South East, I'd estimate tree root intrusion causes over 60% of the recurring blocked drains I attend. The trees are beautiful — but they love your drain pipes."

How Tree Roots Find Their Way Into Pipes

Roots are drawn to moisture and nutrients. Your underground drain pipes are, essentially, a constant water and nutrient source — and any small crack, joint gap or seal failure is an entry point. Once inside, roots don't stop. They grow rapidly inside the pipe, eventually causing partial or complete blockage.

Earthenware (clay) pipes that were common in homes built before the 1980s are particularly vulnerable. The pipe joints were sealed with mortar or rubber that deteriorates over time, opening gaps just large enough for fine root hairs to enter.

Signs You Have Tree Root Blockage

  • Recurring blockages in the same drain
  • Slow drainage throughout the house
  • Gurgling sounds from toilets or drains
  • Sewage smell in the yard
  • Green patches of grass over buried drain lines

How We Fix It

The right fix for tree root blockage depends on the severity. Our process starts with a CCTV camera inspection to see exactly how bad the intrusion is. From there:

  • Minor root intrusion: 5000 PSI hydro jetting cuts through root matter and flushes the pipe clean.
  • Moderate intrusion: Hydro jetting plus root treatment to inhibit regrowth.
  • Severe or collapsed pipe: Pipe relining or replacement may be required.

If your home has old earthenware pipes and mature trees nearby, it's not a matter of if you'll get a tree root blockage — it's when. Preventative CCTV inspections every few years can catch root intrusion before it becomes an emergency.

5 Warning Signs You Have a Blocked Drain

April 2025 · 4 min read · By Jack, Water Serpent Plumbing

Home›Blog›5 Signs of a Blocked Drain

Catch a Blocked Drain Early and Save Money

Most blocked drains don't happen overnight. They develop over weeks or months, giving homeowners plenty of opportunity to catch the problem before it becomes an expensive emergency. Here are the five warning signs every Melbourne homeowner should know.

1. Slow Draining Water

If your sink, shower or bath takes noticeably longer to drain than it used to, this is usually the first sign of a developing blockage. Don't ignore it — slow drainage almost always progresses to a complete blockage if left unaddressed.

2. Gurgling Sounds

Gurgling or bubbling from drains — particularly in the toilet when you run a sink or shower — indicates air trapped in the drain system by a partial blockage. This is a telltale sign and worth investigating promptly.

3. Bad Smells Coming From Drains (Don't Ignore This One)

A sewage or rotten-egg smell from indoor drains means organic matter is decomposing in your pipe — or worse, there's a partial sewage blockage. This is a health concern, not just an inconvenience.

Bad drain smells should never be masked with chemicals or air fresheners. The cause needs to be found and fixed.

4. Water Pooling Around External Drains

After rain, do you notice water pooling in the yard, around the house perimeter, or on the driveway? This often indicates a blocked stormwater or subsurface drain. Left unchecked, this can cause significant foundation and landscaping damage.

5. Multiple Fixtures Slow or Blocked Simultaneously

If two or more drains in your home are slow or blocked at the same time — say, the toilet and the laundry — this usually indicates a main sewer line blockage rather than individual fixture issues. This is more serious and requires professional attention immediately.

What to Do If You Notice These Signs

Hydro Jet vs Drain Snake: Which Actually Works?

March 2025 · 6 min read · By Jack, Water Serpent Plumbing

Home›Blog›Hydro Jet vs Drain Snake

The Honest Difference Between Hydro Jetting and Drain Snaking

When you call a plumber about a blocked drain, the method they use matters enormously. Two plumbers attending the same blocked drain may use very different approaches — and the result can vary from a temporary fix that lasts six months to a proper clear that lasts years.

What Is a Drain Snake?

A drain snake (also called an electric eel or auger) is a long, flexible cable that is fed into the drain pipe and rotated to mechanically break through the blockage. It physically punches a hole through the obstruction — but it does not clean the pipe walls or remove all the material causing the blockage.

What Is Hydro Jetting?

Hydro jetting uses water under extremely high pressure — in our case, 5000 PSI — to flush through the drain pipe. The force is strong enough to cut through tree roots, blast away grease, and scour the pipe walls completely clean. It doesn't just punch through the blockage — it removes it entirely.

"A drain snake is like poking a hole in a clogged bathtub. Hydro jetting is like power washing the inside of the pipe from one end to the other."

Why Hydro Jetting Produces Better Long-Term Results

  • Removes all blockage material rather than just displacing it
  • Cleans pipe walls, eliminating the residue that causes re-blockage
  • Cuts through tree roots rather than just bending around them
  • Works on grease and fat build-up that snakes cannot remove
  • The result can be verified with CCTV camera after jetting

When Might a Drain Snake Be Used?

Drain snakes are appropriate for simple, fresh blockages — a foreign object recently dropped into a drain, or a very superficial soft clog close to the access point. For anything recurring, involving tree roots, grease build-up or whole-house drainage issues, hydro jetting is the correct tool.

"Flushable" Wipes Are Ruining Melbourne's Drains

Home›Blog›Flushable Wipes Problem

The Truth About "Flushable" Wipes

If there is one thing that has dramatically increased the number of blocked toilet and sewer calls across Melbourne in the past decade, it's so-called "flushable" wipes. Despite what the packaging says, these products do not break down in water the way toilet paper does — and they are causing blockages in homes across Melbourne's South East every single week.

Toilet paper is designed to disintegrate in water within seconds. Wet wipes — including baby wipes, facial wipes and products marketed as "flushable" — hold their structure for months or years.

What Happens When You Flush Wipes

A single wipe flushed may pass through the toilet without issue. But wipes accumulate on rough pipe walls, at pipe bends, and at any partial restriction. Over time, they form a dense matted plug that no amount of flushing will shift — and by the time the toilet backs up, the blockage is often significant.

The Simple Solution

Put a small bin next to every toilet. No wipes, nappies, sanitary products or paper towels in the toilet. Toilet paper only. This single change eliminates the most common cause of preventable toilet and sewer blockages in Melbourne homes.

How to Stop Kitchen Grease Blocking Your Drains

September 2024 · 4 min read · By Jack, Water Serpent Plumbing

Home›Blog›Kitchen Grease Drain Blockages

Kitchen Grease: The Slow Killer of Drain Pipes

Kitchen sink blockages are almost entirely preventable — but they remain one of the most common plumbing callouts across Melbourne. The culprit, in most cases, is fat, oil and grease poured down the sink or washed off dishes.

What Happens to Grease in Your Pipes

Hot grease or oil flows easily when warm — but as it travels through the pipe and cools, it solidifies and sticks to pipe walls. Over weeks and months, this build-up narrows the pipe progressively until water can no longer pass. At that point, no amount of running hot water will shift it — it requires professional clearing.

Never pour cooking oil, fat or grease down the sink. Let it cool, pour it into a container, and put it in the bin. This one habit prevents the majority of kitchen drain blockages.

What to Do About Kitchen Grease Build-Up

If your kitchen sink is draining slowly, don't reach for chemical drain cleaners — they rarely fully remove grease and can damage pipes. The right solution is professional hydro jet cleaning, which scours the pipe walls clean. Call Jack on 0425 226 636.
