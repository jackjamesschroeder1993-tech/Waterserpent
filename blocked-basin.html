Blocked Bathroom Basin Repairs Specialists in Small-Bore Drains

A blocked bathroom basin is almost always caused by hair, soap scum and toothpaste building up inside the pop-up waste or the small-bore trap below the vanity. Water Serpent Plumbing clears blocked basins quickly and cleanly without dismantling your vanity unit unless absolutely necessary.

For en-suites, powder rooms and older 32mm waste lines we use specialised micro-augers and compact jet heads designed for tight-radius bends — so we clear the blockage without damaging chrome traps or modern plastic pop-ups.

Common Warning Signs

  • Slow-emptying basin: partial blockage in the trap or pop-up waste.
  • Standing water in the bowl: fully blocked trap or branch waste.
  • Foul smell at the plughole: biofilm and trapped hair starting to decompose.
  • Water leaking under the vanity: pressure from the blockage forcing water past a loose trap connection.
  • Black flecks in the water: biofilm dislodging from a poorly maintained waste — a sign it is time for a proper clean.

Our Diagnostic & Repair Process

  1. Pop-up waste removal: we remove and clean the pop-up assembly which is the most common cause.
  2. Trap clean: P-trap or bottle trap is removed, cleaned and refitted with new washers if needed.
  3. Branch line clearing: if the blockage is further down we use micro-augers or a compact jetter on the 32–40mm waste.
  4. Reassembly & test: all connections retorqued, all seals checked, full flow test before sign-off.

Equipment We Use

  • Micro-augers: specifically designed for 32mm and 40mm waste lines.
  • Compact electric jetters: low-flow, high-pressure clearing safe for chrome and plastic traps.
  • Push-cameras: visual confirmation in cases of recurring blockage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I just pour boiling water down?

Boiling water can damage plastic waste fittings and silicone seals. It also rarely clears a hair-and-soap blockage. Mechanical clearing is much more reliable.

Do I need a new basin or just a clean?

Almost always just a clean and clear. We only recommend replacement if the trap or pop-up is corroded beyond reasonable repair.

Will you make a mess?

No. We use drop sheets, contain the water and clean down after every job. Most basin jobs leave no trace.
