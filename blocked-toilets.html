🪠 Can I Unblock the Toilet Myself First?

Often, yes — and we’d rather you try the safe options before paying anyone. About half the blocked toilets in Melbourne are paper or waste sitting in the pan or trap, which a good plunger can shift. Here’s what works and what to avoid:

MethodGood forThe technique / the catch
🪠 Funnel-cup plungerPaper or waste in the pan or trapGet a full seal over the outlet, push down slowly, pull back sharply — repeat 10–15 times
🧴 Dish soap + warm waterSoft paper blockagesA generous squirt, half a bucket of warm (never boiling) water, wait 20 minutes, try one flush
⚠️ Caustic or chemical cleanersNot recommended in toiletsCan crack porcelain, eat rubber seals and cause dangerous splashback — and they don’t touch roots or wipes
👨‍🔧 Licensed drain plumberRoots, wipes, repeat blockages, multiple fixturesFixed upfront price, pan-safe equipment, CCTV diagnosis and guaranteed work

When to stop: if two rounds of plunging haven’t shifted it, or more than one fixture is backing up, stop — forcing it floods floors and pushes the blockage deeper. For the full step-by-step, read our complete blocked toilet guide for Melbourne homes.

Common Warning Signs

  • Water rising on flush: the pan fills instead of emptying — usually a pan or trap blockage.
  • Slow draining toilet: partial blockage in the trap or downstream pipework.
  • Gurgling from other fixtures when flushing: venting or main-line issue — needs investigating quickly.
  • Foul smell from the bowl: biofilm or trapped material in the pan, or a dry vent.
  • Multiple toilets blocked: almost certainly a main sewer blockage rather than a pan issue.

Our Diagnostic & Repair Process

  1. Toilet inspection: we confirm whether the blockage is in the pan, the S/P trap or further down the line.
  2. Pan-safe clearing tools: we use closet augers and bowl-safe equipment that will not chip or scratch the ceramic.
  3. CCTV if main-line suspected: when more than one fixture is affected we camera-survey the sewer line.
  4. Hydro-jet or eel as required: matched to the pipe diameter and blockage type.
  5. Test, clean & report: we flush-test, clean the work area thoroughly and issue a written invoice.

Equipment We Use

  • Closet augers: pan-safe coated cables that protect porcelain.
  • Push-rod cameras: visual confirmation of the blockage type and location.
  • Mini-jetters: for internal soil pipes and small-bore branch lines.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a blocked toilet plumber near me who can come today?

Yes — if you're anywhere in Melbourne's outer east, the Dandenong Ranges or the Yarra Valley, we offer same-day blocked toilet callouts, including after hours. Stop flushing and call Jack direct on 0425 226 636 for a real arrival time.

Can I keep using my other toilet?

If only one toilet is affected, usually yes. If multiple fixtures are slow or backing up, stop running water immediately and call us — it is almost certainly a main sewer blockage.

Will you damage my toilet?

No. Our technicians use pan-safe equipment specifically designed for ceramic fittings and we carry insurance for the unlikely event of any damage.

My toilet keeps blocking — why?

Recurring blockages usually indicate a structural defect in the soil pipe (root intrusion, offset joints, collapsed section) or an incorrect fall. A CCTV inspection will identify the cause for a permanent fix.

My new toilet keeps blocking — is something wrong with it?

Usually the toilet itself is fine. New water-efficient toilets use less water per flush, so they're less forgiving of a drain line that already has a partial blockage, roots or poor fall. If a brand-new toilet keeps blocking, the problem is almost always downstream in the pipe — we clear and camera the line to find exactly what the new pan has exposed.

Can you unblock a badly clogged toilet today?

Yes — same-day toilet unblocking is our standard, not a premium service. Most clogged toilets are cleared within the hour of arrival, and we always clear the actual cause rather than just plunging the symptom.

Yes — an overflowing toilet is exactly what our 24/7 emergency line is for. Stop flushing, turn off the cistern tap if you can reach it, and call 0425 226 636. Jack answers his own phone, gives you an honest ETA, and attends overflowing toilets across Melbourne's east, the Dandenong Ranges and the Yarra Valley — day, night and weekends.

Do you charge a call-out fee?

No call-out fee within our core service area. You receive a fixed upfront price before any work begins.

How do I unblock a badly blocked toilet myself?

Use a funnel-cup plunger: seal it fully over the outlet, push down slowly and pull back sharply, 10–15 times. For soft paper blockages, dish soap and half a bucket of warm water left for 20 minutes can work. If two rounds of plunging don’t shift it, stop — forcing it floods floors and pushes the blockage deeper.

Why does my toilet gurgle when I flush?

Gurgling is air being pulled through the water seal because the drain or its vent can’t breathe properly — usually a partial blockage forming in the soil pipe or sewer, or a blocked vent. It’s an early warning sign: clearing the line at this stage is quicker and cheaper than waiting for a full overflow.

Should I pour drain cleaner or caustic soda into a blocked toilet?

No. Chemical cleaners rarely fix what actually blocks toilets — paper, wipes and tree roots — the heat they generate can crack porcelain, and they turn the bowl into a splashback hazard for whoever clears it properly afterwards. A plunger or a professional clear is safer and actually works.
