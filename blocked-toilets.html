Blocked Toilet Repairs Same-Day Response

A blocked toilet is more than an inconvenience — left untreated it can flood your bathroom, damage flooring and contaminate the home with raw sewage. Water Serpent Plumbing clears blocked toilets seven days a week with same-day response across Melbourne's eastern suburbs.

We identify whether the blockage is in the pan, the trap, the soil pipe or the main sewer line, and we use the right tool for the job. No mess, no damage to your floor or pan, and a clear written invoice.

Common Warning Signs

  • Water rising on flush: the pan fills instead of emptying — usually a pan or trap blockage.
  • Slow draining toilet: partial blockage in the trap or downstream pipework.
  • Gurgling from other fixtures when flushing: venting or main-line issue — needs investigating quickly.
  • Foul smell from the bowl: biofilm or trapped material in the pan, or a dry vent.
  • Multiple toilets blocked: almost certainly a main sewer blockage rather than a pan issue.

Our Diagnostic & Repair Process

  1. Toilet inspection: we confirm whether the blockage is in the pan, the S/P trap or further down the line.
  2. Pan-safe clearing tools: we use closet augers and bowl-safe equipment that will not chip or scratch the ceramic.
  3. CCTV if main-line suspected: when more than one fixture is affected we camera-survey the sewer line.
  4. Hydro-jet or eel as required: matched to the pipe diameter and blockage type.
  5. Test, clean & report: we flush-test, clean the work area thoroughly and issue a written invoice.

Equipment We Use

  • Closet augers: pan-safe coated cables that protect porcelain.
  • Push-rod cameras: visual confirmation of the blockage type and location.
  • Mini-jetters: for internal soil pipes and small-bore branch lines.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I keep using my other toilet?

If only one toilet is affected, usually yes. If multiple fixtures are slow or backing up, stop running water immediately and call us — it is almost certainly a main sewer blockage.

Will you damage my toilet?

No. Our technicians use pan-safe equipment specifically designed for ceramic fittings and we carry insurance for the unlikely event of any damage.

My toilet keeps blocking — why?

Recurring blockages usually indicate a structural defect in the soil pipe (root intrusion, offset joints, collapsed section) or an incorrect fall. A CCTV inspection will identify the cause for a permanent fix.

Do you charge a call-out fee?

No call-out fee within our core service area. You receive a fixed upfront price before any work begins.
