What Is Pipe Locating & Why Does It Matter?

Pipe locating is the process of tracing underground pipes to find their exact horizontal position and depth below ground — before you dig, before you build, or after a CCTV inspection reveals a fault that needs excavating.

Without it, you're guessing. Builders have destroyed stormwater lines digging footings. Landscapers have cut through sewer mains installing irrigation. Homeowners have spent thousands excavating the wrong spot. A pipe locate costs a fraction of that — and takes an hour.

Most drainage problems found on CCTV need a pipe locate before any repair can happen. We routinely combine both services into a single visit — camera to find the fault, sonde to mark the ground directly above it.

📍 Surface Position

We mark the pipe centreline at ground level with spray chalk so your builder or excavator digs in exactly the right spot — no wider than necessary.

📏 Depth Readings

Our receiver displays pipe depth in real time. We record readings at regular intervals along the run and give you a clear site plan with depths marked.

🔌 All Pipe Materials

Clay, cast iron, copper, PVC, HDPE — we locate them all. Metal pipes via signal clamp. Plastic and clay via sonde transmitter pushed through by CCTV camera or drain rod.

📋 Written Report

On-site sketch plan with pipe route, depth markers and access point locations. Accepted by builders, engineers, councils and insurers.

When Do You Need a Pipe Locate?

  • Before building a deck, extension, carport or pool — confirm drainage routes so your slab footings don't crack a sewer main.
  • After a CCTV inspection finds a fault — know exactly where to excavate rather than guessing from the camera footage.
  • Pre-purchase property inspections — confirm drain routes, depths and condition before you settle on an older Melbourne home.
  • Landscaping and retaining walls — gardens, irrigation trenches and timber sleeper walls all risk striking buried pipes without a locate.
  • Insurance and council applications — many Melbourne councils require a drainage plan showing pipe positions and depths for building permits.
  • Unexplained wet patches or sinkholes — locate the pipe to confirm whether a leaking joint is the cause and exactly where it sits.
  • Strata, commercial and body corporate — trace shared drainage lines to identify ownership boundaries and plan maintenance.

How Our Pipe Locating Service Works

  1. Access the pipe — We enter at the nearest inspection opening: a floor waste, external gully, inspection point or downpipe. On metal pipes we can often trace from above ground using the CAT & Genny signal clamp without entering the pipe at all.
  2. Push the sonde transmitter — For plastic, PVC and clay pipes we feed a battery-powered sonde transmitter through the line using our CCTV camera or a flexible drain rod. The sonde broadcasts a signal at a known frequency that passes through any soil type.
  3. Trace the line at the surface — Walking above the pipe with the receiver, we track the signal and mark the centreline with spray chalk at regular intervals. The receiver shows live depth readings as we walk.
  4. Record depths and mark the site — Depth is logged at each mark. For complex sites we photograph the markings and produce a hand-drawn site plan showing the pipe route relative to structures, boundaries and other services.
  5. Verbal and written handover — We walk you through the results on-site, answer your builder's or excavator's questions if they're present, and leave you with documentation you can use for your project.

Pipe Locating FAQs

How does pipe locating work?

We push a sonde transmitter through your pipe using a CCTV camera or drain rod. A surface receiver picks up the signal and shows the exact ground-level position of the pipe. The same equipment reads depth — typically accurate to within 50mm. For metal pipes we use a CAT and Genny signal clamp to trace the line from above ground without entering the pipe at all.

Can you locate PVC and plastic pipes?

Yes. PVC and plastic can't be traced with a signal clamp but we locate them by pushing a sonde transmitter through the pipe with our CCTV camera. This gives us both position and depth for any pipe material — clay, cast iron, copper, HDPE and PVC.

How deep can you locate pipes?

In typical Melbourne residential conditions our equipment reliably reads to around 3–4 metres depth. Most domestic sewer and stormwater pipes in South East Melbourne sit between 0.6m and 2.5m. We give you a depth reading on-site, marked on the surface so your excavator knows exactly where to dig.

Do you mark the pipe position on the ground?

Yes. We use spray chalk or ground markers to mark the pipe centreline and depth at regular intervals. Your builder, landscaper or excavator digs precisely — no guessing, no risk of striking the pipe.

Can you locate a pipe if I don't know where the access point is?

Often yes. We start at the nearest inspection opening — a floor waste, downpipe or external gully. If there's no obvious access we can flush a locatable sonde down the toilet or create a temporary access point. We'll advise the best approach before committing to any cost.

How much does pipe locating cost in Melbourne?

A standard residential pipe locate starts from $180. Combined CCTV and locate visits start from $320. We provide a fixed written quote before we start — call 0425 226 636 for your specific situation.

How is this different from calling Dial Before You Dig?

Dial Before You Dig gives you council and utility authority records — mains, gas, power. It doesn't cover the private drainage pipes on your property (stormwater, sewer, sub-soil). Our pipe locating service covers exactly that — the pipes on your land that don't appear on any authority plan.
