Signs You Have a Blocked Drain

Catching a blocked drain early can save you thousands. Here's what Melbourne homeowners commonly notice before a full blockage:

  • Gurgling toilet or sink — air escaping past a partial blockage.
  • Slow-draining shower, basin or kitchen sink — usually grease, hair or soap build-up.
  • Bad smells from drains — stagnant water sitting against a blockage. Common in summer.
  • Water backing up in a different fixture (e.g. shower fills when toilet flushes) — main line problem.
  • Wet patches in the garden or sinkholes — collapsed or cracked pipe leaking sewage.
  • Drains overflowing during rain — stormwater connection blocked, often by tree roots.
Tree roots cause more than 60% of blocked sewer drains in Melbourne's South East. Older clay and earthenware pipes (pre-1980s homes in Ringwood, Croydon, Boronia, Ferntree Gully) are especially vulnerable.

What Causes Blocked Drains in Melbourne?

Tree Roots

Liquidambars, gums and willows hunt water. They find hairline cracks in old clay pipes and grow inward. Mechanical cutting clears them temporarily; pipe relining solves it permanently.

Kitchen Grease (FOG)

Fats, oils and grease cool inside pipes and form rock-hard deposits. Hydro jetting is the only proper fix — chemicals don't touch it.

Hair & Soap Scum

The number-one cause of blocked showers and basins. Builds up in P-traps and pipe joins.

Foreign Objects

Wet wipes (even "flushable" ones), sanitary products, kids' toys, food scraps. None of these break down in your pipes.

Collapsed or Cracked Pipes

Soil movement, age, vehicles driven over yard pits. Confirmed with a CCTV inspection and resolved with no-dig pipe relining.

Stormwater Debris

Leaves, silt and roof gutter debris choking stormwater pits — common in Yarra Valley properties surrounded by gums.

Blocked Drain FAQs

How much does it cost to unblock a drain in Melbourne?

Most blocked drains in Melbourne cost between $99 and $450. Simple shower or basin blockages are at the lower end; main sewer lines with tree roots are at the upper end. We offer a $99 fixed-price inspection that includes diagnosis and a written quote.

Should I use drain cleaner from the supermarket first?

No. Caustic drain cleaners damage pipe joins, eat away at older pipes, are dangerous to handle, and rarely clear anything beyond a partial soap clog. They also make our job harder and more expensive when we have to flush the chemical out first.

How long does it take to clear a blocked drain?

Most jobs are done in 60–90 minutes from arrival. Heavily root-blocked main lines can take 2–3 hours. We give you a time estimate before we start.

Will my insurance cover a blocked drain?

Standard home insurance usually doesn't cover gradual blockages. It may cover damage caused by sewage overflow (e.g. flooring, walls). We provide a CCTV report and written quote that insurers accept.
