Blocked Drain Specialists Melbourne Eastern Suburbs

A blocked drain rarely fixes itself. What starts as a slow-draining sink or a gurgling toilet usually escalates into a full backflow, foul odour, or sewage overflow if it is not properly diagnosed. Every job starts with finding the actual cause: tree roots, collapsed pipework, grease build-up, foreign objects, scale, or poor falls. We clear the blockage, then film the line so you can see exactly what was wrong and what we did. No cowboy fixes, no surprise invoices.

What Causes Blocked Drains in Melbourne?

Melbourne's older eastern and outer-eastern suburbs share a particular mix of problems. Mature gums, liquidambars and other thirsty trees push roots into ageing earthenware and clay pipes, while heavy clay soil shifts through the wet–dry seasons and opens up cracks and joints over time. The causes we clear most often are:

  • Tree roots: the number-one cause across the eastern suburbs and Yarra Ranges — roots find a hairline crack and grow into a solid mass that traps everything behind it.
  • Grease, fat and food waste: kitchen build-up that gradually narrows the pipe until flow stops completely.
  • Collapsed or misaligned pipes: common in homes still running their original mid-20th-century earthenware drainage.
  • Foreign objects and wipes: so-called "flushable" wipes, sanitary products and kids' toys that snag and build up over time.
  • Stormwater debris: leaves, silt and sediment that overwhelm pits and downpipes, especially through Melbourne's wetter months.

Common Warning Signs

  • Multiple slow drains: sinks, showers and toilets emptying slowly at the same time almost always means a main sewer or stormwater blockage.
  • Gurgling toilets or sinks: air being forced past a partial blockage as water tries to pass.
  • Foul smells: sewer gas escaping through traps that have lost their water seal.
  • Water backing up: water rising in the lowest fixture (often the laundry trough or downstairs toilet) when another is used.
  • Overflowing inspection points or surcharge gullies: a clear sign the main line is fully blocked and requires urgent attention.

Our Diagnostic & Repair Process

  1. On-site assessment: we map your fixtures, identify the affected line and locate the nearest inspection opening.
  2. CCTV camera inspection: we feed a calibrated drain camera into the line to confirm the blockage type and exact location, including depth and distance from the IO. For recurring blockages or pre-purchase checks, see our dedicated CCTV drain inspection service.
  3. Mechanical or hydro-jet clearing: roots, scale and grease are cut and flushed with the right tool for the pipe material — PVC, earthenware, cast iron or copper.
  4. Post-clear camera verification: we re-film the line so you can see it run free, and we provide footage on request.
  5. Written report & repair options: if we find a structural defect (cracks, offsets, root intrusion) we quote pipe relining or excavation in writing before any further work.

Equipment We Use

  • High-pressure water jetters: up to 5000 PSI for tree roots, fats and heavy scale.
  • Electric eels: for small-bore lines, basins and showers where jetting is overkill.
  • Push-rod & tractor CCTV cameras: self-levelling colour cameras with on-screen distance counters.
  • Pipe locators & sondes: pinpoint the blockage from above-ground to within 100mm.

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly can you clear a blocked drain?

Most blockages are cleared the same day we attend. For emergency calls we aim for a one to two hour response across our core service area.

Do you guarantee your drain clearing?

Yes. Every drain we clear is backed by our workmanship guarantee. If a cleared line blocks again from the same cause within the guarantee period, we return at no cost.

Will you damage my pipes with a jetter?

No. Our technicians match pressure and nozzle type to the pipe material. Hydro-jetting at the correct PSI is safer for old earthenware than mechanical augers.

Do you do after-hours work?

Yes. We run a 24/7 emergency line for sewer overflows, raw sewage in the home, and commercial drain failures.

My drain keeps blocking — why does it keep coming back?

A drain that blocks again and again usually has an underlying cause that clearing alone doesn't fix — most often tree roots growing back through a cracked joint, a section of collapsed or bellied pipe, or poor pipe falls. We run a CCTV camera to find the real reason, then recommend the most cost-effective permanent fix, such as pipe relining, rather than charging you to clear the same blockage every few months.

There's a sewer smell in my house — is that a blocked drain?

A sewer or rotten-egg smell inside the home usually means a drain or vent problem: a dried-out trap that has lost its water seal, a partial blockage letting gas escape, or a cracked pipe or vent. We can smoke-test and CCTV the line to locate the source and stop the smell at the cause, not just mask it.

Why is my drain gurgling or overflowing outside?

Gurgling sinks and toilets, or water surging up an outside gully or inspection point, are classic signs the main sewer or stormwater line is partially or fully blocked. It's best dealt with quickly before it backs up into the house — call us and we'll clear it the same day and camera-check the line.
