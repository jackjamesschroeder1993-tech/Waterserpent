Blocked Shower Drain Repairs Hair, Soap & Grit Removed

Standing in ankle-deep water mid-shower is not how anyone wants to start the day. A blocked shower drain is almost always caused by hair, soap residue and skin cells binding into a matted clog inside the grate or the trap below. Water Serpent Plumbing clears blocked showers same-day, including built-in linear drains and tiled wet-room floors.

We carry specialised tools that allow us to clear shower wastes through the grate without lifting tiles — protecting your bathroom finish and your warranty.

Common Warning Signs

  • Water pooling around your feet: partial blockage just below the grate.
  • Slow draining shower: restricted flow through the trap.
  • Smell from the floor waste: biofilm in the trap or a dry seal allowing sewer gas back in.
  • Water backing up in the bath when the shower runs: shared branch line blocked further downstream.
  • Floor staining at the threshold: a chronic slow-drain finally leaking past the silicone.

Our Diagnostic & Repair Process

  1. Grate lift & inspection: we lift the grate, photograph what we find, and document before/after.
  2. Hair-tool extraction: specialist hair-pulling tools remove the matted mass without scratching the trap.
  3. Branch-line clear: if the blockage is past the trap we use a compact eel or micro-jetter.
  4. Bio-flush: we treat the line with an enzyme cleaner to break down residual soap scum.
  5. Re-seat grate: grate is cleaned, re-bedded if loose and pressure-tested.

Equipment We Use

  • Hair-extraction barbed tools: remove the mat in one pull without lifting tiles.
  • Compact electric jetters: safe for 50mm and 80mm shower wastes.
  • Mini push cameras: verify clearance in recurring-blockage cases.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will you have to lift my tiles?

Almost never. In over 95% of jobs we clear the blockage through the existing grate. Tile lifting is only needed where the floor waste itself is broken.

My linear drain is blocked — can you clear it?

Yes. We service all common linear and tile-insert drains including Stormtech, Vincent Buda and Lauxes.

How do I stop it happening again?

A simple hair-strainer over the grate and a monthly enzyme treatment will keep a shower waste running freely indefinitely.
