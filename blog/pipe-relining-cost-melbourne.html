Pipe Relining Cost Melbourne: 2026 Price Guide

June 2026 · 7 min read · By Jack Schroeder, Water Serpent Plumbing

Home›Blog›Pipe Relining Cost Melbourne

Short answer: Most pipe relining jobs in Melbourne cost between $3,000 and $15,000, depending on how much pipe needs relining and how accessible it is. As a rough guide, expect around $2,000–$3,000 for the first metre and $400–$1,000 for each metre after that. Because there's no digging, relining is usually 30–50% cheaper than excavating and replacing the same pipe. The only way to get an exact figure is a CCTV inspection — which tells us the precise length, location and condition of the damage.

What You'll Pay, at a Glance

These are realistic guide ranges for Melbourne's east in 2026 — not fixed quotes, because every pipe is different:

  • Short section reline (1–3 metres): $2,500 – $5,000
  • Standard residential reline (4–10 metres): $5,000 – $12,000
  • Larger or harder-access reline: $12,000 – $15,000+
  • Per-metre rate after the first metre: $400 – $1,000
  • Full excavation & pipe replacement (for comparison): $15,000 – $50,000+

Your actual price depends on the factors below, which is why we always run a camera first and give you a fixed price before any work starts.

What Changes the Price

A few things move pipe relining cost up or down:

  • Length of pipe. The first metre carries the setup cost; additional metres are cheaper per metre.
  • Pipe diameter. Standard 100mm residential pipe costs less than 150mm-plus lines, which need more material.
  • Access. A pipe reached through an existing inspection point is far cheaper than one under a slab, driveway or deep underground.
  • Condition. Heavy root intrusion or a partial collapse may need extra cleaning or a patch before relining.
  • Bends and junctions. More complex pipe runs take longer and cost more.

Why Relining Is Cheaper Than Replacing

Traditional repair means digging up the pipe — and then paying again to restore the garden, driveway, paving or flooring on top. That excavation and reinstatement is where the real cost lives, which is why full replacement commonly runs $15,000–$50,000+.

Pipe relining avoids all of that. We insert a resin liner inside the existing pipe and cure it in place, creating a smooth, joint-free, root-resistant new pipe without digging. No excavation means no landscaping bill, far less disruption, and a job that's often finished in a day. It also typically carries a long structural warranty. If you want to weigh the two approaches side by side, see our pipe relining vs replacement guide.

"I never quote relining blind. The camera goes in first so you know exactly what you're paying for — the real length, the real damage, and a fixed price before I start. No surprises halfway through."

When Relining Is the Right Call (and When It Isn't)

Relining is ideal for cracked, leaking or root-invaded pipes that are still structurally mostly intact — which covers the majority of repeat blocked-drain offenders in Melbourne's older suburbs like Ringwood, Croydon and Ferntree Gully. If a pipe has completely collapsed or is badly misaligned, replacement may be the only option. A CCTV inspection tells us which camp you're in, so you're never paying for the wrong fix.
