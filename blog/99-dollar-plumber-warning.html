Why the $99 Blocked Drain Plumber Could Cost You Thousands

May 2025 · 6 min read · By Jack, Water Serpent Plumbing

The $99 Blocked Drain Special: What They're Not Telling You

You've seen the ads. "$99 Blocked Drain Cleared!" It sounds like a bargain — especially when you've got an overflowing drain and you need help fast. But as a plumber with over 20 years of experience, I've cleaned up after these operators more times than I can count. Here's the honest truth about what's really going on.

"No qualified, properly equipped plumber can attend your home, assess the problem, use professional equipment, and fix it properly for $99. The maths simply doesn't work — and someone always pays the difference. Usually you."

The Reality Behind the $99 Price

Let's be straight about what $99 covers in the real world. A licensed plumber's hourly rate in Melbourne sits well above that figure before you factor in running a vehicle, fuel, tools, insurance, licensing and compliance costs. The moment a qualified tradesperson walks out the door to attend a job, the cost is already more than $99.

So how do these companies advertise it? Simple: the $99 is a call-out and assessment fee designed to get someone in your door. Once they're there, the upselling begins.

The Bait-and-Switch Playbook

Here's what typically happens when a "$99 drain" operator arrives at your home:

  • Step 1 — The assessment: They have a look and immediately identify "additional complications" that push the price well beyond $99. Suddenly it's $350, $500 or more.
  • Step 2 — The urgency pressure: You're told the problem is worse than it looks and needs fixing right now. You're already stressed about the blockage, it's hard to say no.
  • Step 3 — The minimal fix: Even if you pay the inflated price, the work done is often the bare minimum. A quick snake through the drain to temporarily clear the blockage — without addressing the underlying cause.
  • Step 4 — The return visit: Because the root cause wasn't fixed, the blockage returns within weeks or months. You call again. The cycle repeats.

Why Cheap Operators Can't Fix the Real Problem

Properly diagnosing and clearing a blocked drain requires equipment — and quality equipment costs money. A CCTV drain camera to inspect the pipe and identify the actual cause of the blockage. A high-pressure hydro jetter to fully clear root intrusion, grease build-up and debris rather than just punching a hole through it. Pipe locating equipment for accurate diagnosis.

Budget operators often carry none of this. They have a basic drain snake — a tool that physically pushes through a blockage without clearing the pipe walls, without addressing root systems, and without any way to confirm the cause or the quality of the result.

The drain appears to flow after they leave. But the root system is still there. The grease build-up is still coating the pipe walls. The partial collapse causing recurring blockages hasn't been identified. It's a temporary clearance, not a fix.

The Hidden Cost of Cheap Plumbing

Consider the full picture. You pay $99 as a call-out. Then another $400 because of "complications." The drain blocks again six weeks later — another $200 to a different operator. Three months later it's completely blocked again, and this time a camera inspection reveals a tree root intrusion and cracked pipe section that should have been identified in the first visit. Now you're looking at pipe relining or excavation — a job that might cost $3,000–$8,000.

Compare that to a properly equipped plumber who charges a fair, transparent price from the start — uses a CCTV camera to correctly diagnose the issue, clears it properly with a hydro jetter, and tells you honestly what the pipe condition is and what to watch for. The upfront cost is higher. The long-term cost is significantly lower.

What to Look for in a Drain Plumber

  • BPC Registration — check the Plumbing Permit Authority Victoria (PPAV) register. Every plumber working in Victoria must be BPC registered. Ask for their registration number.
  • Proper equipment — do they carry a CCTV drain camera and hydro jetter? If not, they can't properly diagnose or fully clear most blockages.
  • Upfront, transparent pricing — a reputable plumber tells you the full cost before starting work. Not a "$99 special" that doubles on arrival.
  • Owner-operated or identifiable tradesperson — who actually turns up matters. An experienced plumber, not a subcontractor or an apprentice.
  • No pressure tactics — a good plumber assesses the situation and gives you honest options. They don't create urgency or pressure you into decisions on the spot.

Why I'm Telling You This

I run Water Serpent Plumbing because I got sick of seeing homeowners ripped off — either by operators who charge for work they didn't do properly, or by cheap services that set customers up for repeat callouts. When I attend a job in Ringwood, Bayswater, Ferntree Gully or anywhere across Melbourne's South East, I bring the right equipment, I do the job properly, and I give you an honest picture of what's happening with your drains.

My pricing is fair and transparent. It's not $99. But it won't leave you calling someone else in a month either.

If you're comparing plumbers and one price looks dramatically cheaper than the others — ask yourself why. Then ask them what's included, whether they carry a CCTV camera, and whether the quoted price is the final price. Their answers will tell you everything you need to know.
