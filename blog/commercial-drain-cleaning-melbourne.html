Commercial Drain Cleaning Melbourne: Property Manager Guide

March 2025 · 5 min read · By Jack, Water Serpent Plumbing

Home›Blog›Commercial Drain Cleaning Melbourne: Property Manager Guide

What Property Managers Need to Know About Drain Maintenance

If you manage residential investment properties or commercial premises across Melbourne's South East, drainage problems are almost inevitable at some point. How you handle them — and how quickly — makes the difference between a minor maintenance issue and a serious damage or liability event.

Why Investment Properties Have More Drain Problems

Rental properties tend to have higher occupancy rates and less attentive use than owner-occupied homes. Tenants flush wet wipes, hair doesn't get cleared from shower drains, and kitchen grease builds up faster with multiple residents. Combined with older pipe infrastructure in established Melbourne suburbs, this creates a higher frequency of drain blockages.

The Property Manager's Response Problem

When a tenant calls about a blocked drain, you need a plumber who actually answers the phone, attends the same day, fixes the problem properly on the first visit, and provides clear documentation. A plumber who uses a basic drain snake to temporarily clear a blockage and bills $200 looks like a bargain — until the drain blocks again three weeks later and the tenant calls again.

The most common mistake property managers make with drain issues is choosing the cheapest option for the first visit. A CCTV inspection during the initial callout identifies whether the issue is a recurring structural problem — saving multiple future callouts and protecting the investment property.

What to Look for in a Commercial Drain Plumber

  • Same-day response availability for tenant callouts
  • CCTV inspection capability — not just a drain snake
  • Written reports with photos and footage for your records
  • Transparent invoicing with no hidden charges
  • Ability to identify recurring structural issues early

Pre-Lease Drain Inspections

Before a new tenancy, a CCTV drain inspection documents the existing condition of the drainage system. This protects you if a tenant causes damage, establishes a baseline for future disputes, and identifies any existing issues before they become the property manager's emergency.
